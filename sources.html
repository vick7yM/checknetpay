Our Sources

Every tax rate, bracket threshold, and contribution limit on CheckNetPay traces to an official government publication. This page documents them all. Last updated: June 2026.

Why Sources Matter for Tax Calculators

Tax rates change every year. A calculator using stale data can produce numbers that look plausible but are wrong — leading users to budget incorrectly or misjudge salary negotiations. We publish this page so you can verify every number we use against its original government source. If you find a discrepancy, let us know.

Federal Tax Sources

Primary Federal Source

IRS Revenue Procedure 2025-32

The official IRS inflation-adjustment publication for tax year 2026. Establishes all federal income tax bracket thresholds, the standard deduction, the AMT exemption, and dozens of other inflation-adjusted figures. This is the authoritative source for 2026 federal income tax parameters.

Key 2026 figures from this document:

  • Single standard deduction: $16,100
  • MFJ standard deduction: $32,200
  • HOH standard deduction: $24,150
  • 10% bracket top (single): $12,400
  • 12% bracket top (single): $50,400
  • 22% bracket top (single): $105,700
  • 24% bracket top (single): $201,775
View Rev. Proc. 2025-32 (PDF) →
Withholding

IRS Publication 15 (Circular E) — Employer's Tax Guide 2026

The IRS's official guide to federal income tax withholding. Contains the withholding tables employers use to determine per-paycheck federal income tax deductions. Used to cross-verify our per-paycheck calculations match IRS withholding expectations.

Self-Employment

IRS Schedule SE and Publication 334

Governs self-employment tax calculation: the 15.3% rate (12.4% SS + 2.9% Medicare), the 92.35% net earnings multiplier, and the above-the-line deduction for half of SE tax. Source for our 1099 calculator mode.

Supplemental Wages

IRS Publication 15-A — Employer's Supplemental Tax Guide

Source for the supplemental wage withholding rate (22% flat rate for bonuses and supplemental pay up to $1 million; 37% above $1 million) used in our bonus calculator.

IRS Publication 15-A →
Additional Medicare

IRS Notice 2013-45 / IRC Section 3101(b)(2)

Establishes the 0.9% Additional Medicare Tax on wages above $200,000 (single) or $250,000 (MFJ). This provision has been in effect since 2013 and confirmed as applicable for 2026. We apply this surcharge to all relevant calculations — a detail many calculators omit.

State Tax Sources

For each state, we use the official state Department of Revenue or Department of Taxation publication. Where a state has released a 2026 withholding guide, we use that document. Where 2026 rates are established by statute but withholding guides are not yet published, we use the legislated rate.

California Franchise Tax Board — 2026 Withholding Schedules

Source for California's progressive income tax brackets, the $5,202 standard deduction (single, 2026), and the 1.1% SDI rate. CA FTB Publication 1016 and the DE-4 withholding method.

California FTB →
New York

New York State Department of Taxation and Finance — IT-2104

Source for New York State's progressive brackets ($8,000 single standard deduction, rates 4%–10.9%), and New York City income tax rates (3.078%–3.876%).

NY Tax Department →
Texas / Florida / Nevada / Washington

No State Income Tax — Constitutional and Statutory Provisions

Texas: Texas Constitution Article 8, §24 (no income tax without voter approval). Florida: Florida Constitution Article VII, §5 (banned since 1924). Nevada: Nevada Constitution Article 10, §1. Washington: No income tax on wages by longstanding court interpretation of WA Constitution Article VII, §1.

How We Handle Conflicting Sources

Occasionally, different sources report slightly different bracket thresholds for the same year — particularly in the period between the IRS publication date and when secondary sources update their databases. Our policy: the original IRS Revenue Procedure or state agency publication always takes precedence over third-party aggregators, tax software vendors, or news articles. If we find a discrepancy, we use the primary government source and note the conflict in our internal review log.

Reporting Errors

If you believe any figure on CheckNetPay is inconsistent with an official source, please email hello@checknetpay.com with:

  • The specific salary, state, and filing status that produced the unexpected result
  • What our calculator showed
  • What you believe the correct figure is, with a link to the source

We investigate all reported discrepancies within 48 hours and publish corrections immediately when errors are confirmed.
